# Hiring Manager Interview — AI Prompts

AI can meaningfully improve your interview preparation — if you use it to go deeper, not just to generate generic answers. These prompts are designed to be adapted to a specific company and role, not used as-is.

Replace every `[bracket]` before running the prompt.

---

## Before the Interview

### 1. Company Research

> "I have an interview with [company] for the position of [job title]. Analyze their mission, core products or services, recent news, and any strategic shifts in the last 12 months. Identify what challenges they're likely trying to solve and what a PM in this role would be expected to prioritize."

This gives you more than a summary — it gives you a strategic frame for how to position yourself in the conversation.

### 2. Cover Letter

> "Based on this job description for [job title] at [company], write a cover letter that connects my experience in [your background] to their stated priorities. Avoid generic language. Focus on what's specific to this company and role."

Review it critically before sending — AI cover letters tend to be polished but flat. Add a specific, personal reason why you want this role.

### 3. Resume Tailoring

> "Review this job description and identify the 5–7 most important skills or experiences the role requires. Then look at my resume and tell me: which of those do I demonstrate clearly, which do I demonstrate weakly, and which are missing? Suggest specific rewrites for the weak areas."

This is more useful than asking AI to "improve" your resume in the abstract.

---

## Preparing for the Interview

### 4. Industry Trends and Challenges

> "For the position of [job title] at [company] in the [industry] sector, what are the 3–5 most important challenges or trends I should be prepared to discuss? What would make a strong answer look like in an interview context?"

### 5. Common Interview Questions

> "Generate a list of the 10 most likely interview questions for a [job title] role at a [company type, e.g., B2B SaaS, fintech, marketplace]. Include both behavioral and product-sense questions."

### 6. Behavioral Questions with STAR Prep

> "Create 5 behavioral interview questions relevant to [job title] at [company]. For each one, suggest how to structure a strong answer using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result), tailored to someone with experience in [your background]."

### 7. Technical or Skills Assessment Practice

> "I need to demonstrate [skill, e.g., SQL for funnel analysis, writing user stories, running a prioritization framework] for a PM role at [company]. Give me 3 realistic practice exercises at the level of rigor this type of company would expect."

---

## After the Interview

### 8. Follow-Up Email

> "Draft a follow-up email for after my interview for [job title] at [company]. The email should thank them for their time, reference one specific topic we discussed ([topic]), reiterate my interest, and briefly state how my background in [area] addresses the challenge we talked about."

Don't send the AI draft verbatim. Personalize it with a specific detail from the conversation.

---

## Salary Negotiation

### 9. Salary Research and Negotiation Framing

> "What is the market rate for a [job title] with [X years] of experience in [location/remote]? Give me a realistic range and the key factors that push toward the higher end. Then suggest 3 ways to frame a salary negotiation that don't start with an apology."

### 10. LinkedIn Outreach

> "Write a short LinkedIn message (under 150 words) to send to a [job title] currently working at [company]. I'm interviewing there for [role] and want to learn about the team culture and how they actually work day-to-day — not information I can get from the website. Make the message specific and respectful of their time."
